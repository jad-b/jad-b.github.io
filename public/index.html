Multi-[_blank_] Modeling

Last updated on Thu, Jul 28, 2016

Multilevel models are statistical models of parameters that vary at more than one level. - source: [Multilevel model:wikipedia]. This implies that the same parameters can take different values amongst sibling models of different parents. To illustrate, let’s work through the example given in wikipedia: A basic linear regression model for predicting income may take age, class, gender, and race as parameters: $f(age,class,gender,race) = income$. However, this is likely to predict the same income for a 45-year old black man in Seattle, WA as it would for one in Mobile, AL.
